5Gum Rebrand — 2026

5GUM
REBRAND

As part of a design project, I reimagined 5Gum with full creative freedom - returning to the sensory promise the brand originally made, but never fully delivered. The new and improved 5Gum rebrand really hones in on all of the 5 senses with tactile packaging, and company collabs that truly make “stimulate your senses” come to life. This project included developing entirely new branding, marketing and packaging concepts whilst using AI tools to help visualize and bring them to life.

Flavour as a sensory system

The 5Gum rebrand reframes the brand around a core idea: flavour isn't just a taste — it's a full sensory experience. Each flavour has its own tactile texture, visual language, and identity system that translates across packaging, social media, and immersive brand moments.

Service Design Uno — 2025

SERVICE
DESIGN UNO

As part of a group project, we were challenged to design a physical card game inspired by the principles of Service Design for Business by Ben Reason. Rather than simply treating learning service design as something purely theoretical, we set out to make it feel practical, engaging, and actually usable.

Service Design Uno is the result — a reimagined take on a nostalgic classic that transforms abstract concepts into something you can actively play with. By blending the familiar gameplay of UNO with real-world thinking, we created an experience that's not only fun, brain teasing, and competitive, but encourages players to actually think like service designers.

Product Design Card Game Service Design Educational Team Project

Overview

HOW IT WORKS

As part of our project requirements, we were tasked with creating a professional-quality, card-based educational game that translates the core concepts of Service Design for Business by Ben Reason into an interactive format. We had full creative freedom with our design and decided that it needed to support 3–8 players, run for 30–45 minutes, and be easy enough for the players to pick up the content quickly and apply the concepts throughout the game.

Service Design Uno follows the familiar mechanics of UNO, but introduces a key twist: every card played must be explained. Players are required to justify their moves using service design thinking, turning each turn into a moment of discussion, reasoning, and application.

The game is built around three card types — foundations, challenges, and tools — allowing players to connect core principles to real-world scenarios. By combining structured gameplay with open-ended thinking, the card game experience encourages players to actively engage with service design concepts rather than passively learn them.

Contribution

ROLE AND IMPACT

As part of a collaborative team of 7 over several weeks, I actively participated in ideation and helped translate service design concepts into clear, actionable directions for the cards. For example, with the development of the Foundation cards, I proposed creating a "main category" for each service design pillar, which made the game more intuitive and ensured the learning objectives were front and center for players. I also focused on shaping the overall direction of the game, refining how service design rules and principles could be integrated into gameplay, and making sure the experience felt engaging and easy to follow.

I played a key role in developing the visual and written components — creating front to back instruction manuals that explain how the game is played, including example exchanges for each card category. I was also in charge of designing the physical card box, including the layout, visuals, and a back-of-the-box summary that clearly communicates the game's objectives, rules, and learning goals, making it immediately accessible and inviting for new players.

LoadUP! — 2025

LOADUP!

A mobile platform designed to solve the frustrations of shared laundry in student housing — real-time availability, virtual queues, and seamless digital payment.

UX Design Mobile App Solo Project Prototype

Overview

The Problem

Students in shared housing constantly face the same frustrations — walking to the laundry room only to find every machine occupied, waiting with no sense of how long a cycle has left, and scrambling for coins or dealing with unreliable card readers. LoadUP was designed to eliminate all three pain points in one seamless app.

Team Size
4 People
Type
Mobile App
Stage
Prototype

App mockup coming soon

Solution

Core Features

LoadUP's solution focuses on three core features that directly address the pain points identified through user research and validated by surveys and interviews with students in shared housing.

01
Live Visibility
Real-time machine status including cycle times — so students always know what's available before leaving their room.
02
Virtual Queue
Reserve a spot in line without standing in the laundry room. Push notifications alert you when your machine is ready.
03
Digital Payment
Integrated payment methods eliminate coins and finicky card readers — a Reserve & Pay workflow that locks machines for users.

Research

Meet Luke

To ground our design decisions, we validated our research with surveys and interviews, then developed a primary persona — Luke, a busy second-year student who values efficiency and predictable routines. Luke hates uncertainty. He just wants to know when a machine is free, reserve it, and move on with his day.

Design Approach

Useful, Usable, Desirable

Throughout the design process, we applied three core principles to every decision:

Useful
Solving the core problem — students need to know machine availability without physically checking.
Usable
Providing clear feedback — intuitive dashboard, status indicators, and queue system that anyone can pick up instantly.
Desirable
Creating an emotionally pleasant experience — turning a frustrating chore into something efficient and stress-free.
